This new environment has different resources and different competitors, so the new population will need different characteristics or adaptations to be a stronger competitor than what they needed before.
The initial population hasn't changed at all; it needs the same adaptations as before.
As time passes and the new population adapts to the environment, its members begin to look increasingly less similar to the other population.
Eventually, after thousands or even millions of years, the two populations will look so different from each other that we will no longer be able to say they are the same species.
